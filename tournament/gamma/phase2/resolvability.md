## 5 · VERDICT — is D-021 a grid *law* or a *gauge/coordinate artifact*?

**D-021 is a GAUGE × DISCRETIZATION ARTIFACT, not a grid law — with a bounded, honest exception at the finest accuracy.**

**The evidence, graded:**

- **EVIDENCE-GRADE (measured, reproduced this session):** the ceiling D-021 hit is a property of **fixed *algebraic-order* (2nd-order FD) discretization**, not of fixed grids as a class. `cheb_convergence` (blake2b `b6ad2eba…`, re-run byte-identical) shows a fixed *spectral* grid reaches 1.6e-9 where the incumbent's O(N⁻²) FD (measured order −1.977, = D-021's own scaling) would need **9,722× the DOF**. Change the discretization on the *same* fixed grid and the ceiling drops ~4 orders of magnitude. **The CPU budget was never the wall; the discretization order was.** *(Caveat carried honestly: this is a proxy Gaussian, not the EKG system; and the same experiment's cusp panel, order −0.68, shows the advantage vanishes at a bare singularity — the artifact lifts only where the field is smooth-on-grid.)*

- **LITERATURE-PROVEN:** the ceiling is also a property of the incumbent's **singularity-avoiding polar-areal slicing** (central lapse hangs where the physics is). **Garfinkle 1995** (abstract verified verbatim) saw the self-similar *echoing* on a **non-adaptive null grid**; **arXiv 2008.12726** reached mass-scaling/DSS on a **single fixed grid** via a concentrating gauge. Change the *gauge* on a fixed grid and the echo is resolvable. **D-021's corpse is scoped precisely to "uniform FD × polar-areal," exactly as the corpse reason literally states** ("a fixed grid caps the self-similar central curvature … refining goes chaotic") — and that scoping is *correct*: it is a statement about *those two choices*, and it does not transfer to spectral discretization or to null/similarity/concentrating gauges.

- **THE HONEST EXCEPTION (why it's an artifact but not a free lunch):** the *echoing* is decisively an artifact (lifts under any coordinate change). But **γ to the CHARTER's <0.05 accuracy** is a stronger demand, and the historical record is unambiguous that **the exponent has always cost *either* refinement *or* a coordinate change that makes the solution stationary/smooth on the fixed grid**: Choptuik used AMR; Hamadé–Stewart needed characteristic AMR *even in null coordinates* "to obtain the accuracy necessary"; Gundlach got γ=0.374±0.001 by the eigenvalue route (a coordinate change that makes γ a converging eigenvalue, no near-critical collapse at all). **No purely non-adaptive fixed-grid method is literature-proven to hit γ<0.05 for this PDE.** So D-021 is not a law — but its replacement is: *the exponent crown at N0 is reachable only by (a) the eigenvalue route in similarity coordinates [CSS], (b) a spectral DOF-density that a smoothness-holding map keeps convergent [spectral], or (c) refinement [AMR, which likely busts the ≤5-min box]. Naive "put the grid on the light cone and fit BH masses" [non-adaptive double-null] gets the echo, not γ.*

**One-sentence ruling for phase 3:** **D-021 correctly killed uniform-FD-on-polar-areal and correctly deferred γ — but it killed a *specific gauge×discretization*, not the crown; the crown is resolvable at N0 by changing what you discretize (spectral) or what you solve (a converging eigenvalue in similarity coordinates, CSS), and the measured 9,722× DOF gap is the receipt that proves the ceiling was never a law of grids.**
